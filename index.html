The Ayurvedic View of Piles

Understanding Arsha (Piles) the Ayurvedic Way

Ayurveda calls piles "Arsha" — a word that literally means enemy because of how deeply this condition disrupts everyday life. From an Ayurvedic standpoint, piles are not just a local rectal problem. They are a sign of chronic digestive imbalance, specifically weakened digestive fire (Mandagni) and aggravated Vata-Pitta doshas.

Shushka Arsha (Dry Piles)

Dominated by Vata and Kapha. No bleeding, but intense pain, itching, and discomfort during and after bowel movements.

Ardra Arsha (Bleeding Piles)

Dominated by Pitta. Characterised by bright red bleeding, a burning sensation, and inflammation of rectal tissue.

Know More From Doctor

What Symptoms Should You Not Ignore?

Many people dismiss early piles symptoms as a temporary problem. Watch for these signs:

Pain or Discomfort

Usually felt during bowel movements or while sitting. It can range from a dull ache to sharp, stinging pain.

Painless Bleeding

Noticing bright red blood on the toilet tissue or in the bowl. This is often the first visible sign of internal piles.

Swelling near Anus

A lump near the anus that may be sensitive or painful, especially during straining or prolonged sitting.

Chronic Constipation

Often a primary cause and a symptom. Straining due to hard stools significantly worsens haemorrhoidal conditions.

Painful Defecation

Experiencing sharp or burning pain specifically during or immediately after passing stool, often due to tissue irritation.

Anal Tenderness

General sensitivity or soreness in the region that makes sitting or physical activity uncomfortable throughout the day.

Understanding the Origin

The Root Causes of Piles

Piles are often the result of long-term digestive imbalances. Identifying the source is the first step towards permanent relief.

It Usually Starts in the Gut

Chronic constipation is the single most common trigger. Straining repeatedly puts excessive pressure on rectal veins, causing them to swell and inflame over time.

Modern Diet Risks

Diets like keto or extreme fasting can increase gut dryness (Vata). Ayurveda focuses on sustainable, balanced nutrition to strengthen your digestive fire (Mandagni).

The "Ama" Factor

When digestion weakens, the body accumulates toxins (Ama), leading to hard stools and repeated strain. Correcting this fire is our first clinical priority.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are piles (hemorrhoids)?
 Piles (hemorrhoids) are vascular structures in the anal canal. Under normal conditions, they act as cushions that help with stool control. They become problematic when swollen or inflamed, leading to symptoms.
Can piles be permanently cured without surgery?
In most early and moderate cases, yes. Ayurvedic treatments like Kshara Karma, herbal medicines, and Panchakarma help treat piles at the root — improving digestion, relieving constipation, and balancing doshas.
How does Triphala Guggul help?
Triphala Guggul supports smooth bowel movements, reduces inflammation in rectal veins, strengthens tissue, and naturally softens stools without dependency risk.
Is turmeric milk effective for pain?
Yes, turmeric is a natural anti-inflammatory that helps reduce internal swelling and ease pain over time.
How long does treatment take?
Most patients see meaningful improvement within 2-4 weeks. Complete healing typically takes 6-12 weeks depending on the grade of Piles.
What are common symptoms of piles?
Common symptoms include bright red bleeding during defecation, anal itching, pain, and feeling a lump near the anus.
