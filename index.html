The Ayurvedic View of Piles

Understanding Arsha (Piles) the Ayurvedic Way

Ayurveda calls piles "Arsha" — a word that literally means enemy because of how deeply this condition disrupts everyday life. From an Ayurvedic standpoint, piles are not just a local rectal problem. They are a sign of chronic digestive imbalance, specifically weakened digestive fire (Mandagni) and aggravated Vata-Pitta doshas.

Shushka Arsha (Dry Piles)

Dominated by Vata and Kapha. No bleeding, but intense pain, itching, and discomfort during and after bowel movements.

Ardra Arsha (Bleeding Piles)

Dominated by Pitta. Characterised by bright red bleeding, a burning sensation, and inflammation of rectal tissue.

What Symptoms Should You Not Ignore?

Many people dismiss early piles symptoms as a temporary problem. Watch for these signs:

Bleeding

Bright red bleeding during bowel movements, especially in internal piles. Even if painless, it should never be ignored.

Pain

Pain during or after passing stool can signal inflamed external piles, thrombosis, or advanced irritation.

Swelling

Swelling or a feeling of a lump near the anus becomes more noticeable during straining or sitting.

Itching

Persistent anal itching can occur due to irritation, inflammation, moisture, or mucus discharge.

Incomplete Evacuation

A constant sensation that bowel movement is unfinished may indicate swelling or internal obstruction.

Discharge

Mucus discharge with stool may accompany irritation, prolapse, or long-standing piles.

Other Symptoms: Painless bleeding | Anal itching | Constipation or painful defecation

The Root Causes of Piles

It Usually Starts in the Gut

Lifestyle and Dietary Triggers

Chronic constipation is the single most common trigger for piles. Straining repeatedly over months and years puts excessive pressure on rectal veins and eventually, they swell and inflame. Other common causes include a diet low in fibre, insufficient water intake, excessive consumption of spicy and fried foods, long hours of sitting without movement, and irregular bowel habits. Pregnancy, obesity, and a family history of haemorrhoids also increase risk significantly.

Modern Diet Risks

Diets like keto, low-carb eating plans, and aggressive intermittent fasting are often very low in fibre and increase gut dryness directly worsening constipation and piles. Ayurveda does not support crash diets or extreme calorie restriction for patients with digestive disorders. Sustainable, balanced nutrition is always the foundation of gut health.

At the core of all these causes is Mandagni (impaired digestive fire). When digestion weakens, the body accumulates toxins (Ama), stools become hard and irregular, and the rectal region comes under repeated strain. Correcting Mandagni is step one of every piles treatment at Healing Earth.

Ayurvedic Lifestyle Tips (Vihara) for Piles Relief

Ayurveda emphasises that healing piles (Arsha) is not just about medicines, it’s about correcting daily habits. Simple lifestyle changes can significantly reduce strain on the rectal area, improve digestion, and prevent recurrence. Avoiding long hours of sitting, responding to natural bowel urges without delay, and maintaining regular meal and sleep timings play a crucial role in recovery. Gentle physical activity, including walking and specific yoga postures, helps improve blood circulation and bowel movement.
